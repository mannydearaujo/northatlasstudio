# Voice Extraction Checklist

Work through each dimension with the source material open. Capture verbatim examples, not summaries.

## Register & rhythm
- Formal or casual? Contractions? First person singular ("I") or plural ("we")?
- Short punchy sentences or longer explanatory ones? (Count words per sentence in owner-authored texts.)
- Do they ask the reader questions, or state things?

## Vocabulary
- Words they use for their own services (may differ from industry terms — "cut & drop" vs "custom fabrication").
- Words CUSTOMERS use in reviews (these carry search intent — keep even if technically imprecise).
- Regional/community markers they use naturally (town names, "the Ave," local events).
- Portuguese/Spanish/other-language phrases if the owner uses them with customers.

## Attitude markers
- How do they talk about price? (Defensive, matter-of-fact, proud-premium?) Capture their exact framing.
- How do they handle complaints/negative topics?
- What do they brag about, and what do they refuse to claim?
- Humor: present? What kind? (Never inject humor that isn't in the source.)

## The refusals (feeds banned-phrases.md)
- Phrases the owner explicitly hates.
- Claims they can't back up.
- Competitor language they don't want echoed.
- Client-specific rules already established (check the operating manual — e.g., Golden Paws bans "one dog at a time" and em-dashes site-wide).

## Proof inventory (feeds proof-points.md)
- Years in business, licenses, insurance, certifications — with verification status.
- Real differentiators (equipment, guarantees, response time) — confirmed how?
- Review themes: what do 3+ reviewers independently praise? That's a proof point with receipts.
