# Review Reply Playbook

Every review gets a reply within 7 days. Replies are written in the owner's voice (voice pack) and approved by the owner before posting.

## 5★ / 4★ — specific thanks
Pattern: thank by name → echo ONE specific detail they mentioned → soft welcome-back. Vary structure; identical replies read as automation.
> "Thanks Sarah! Bella's doodle coat takes real patience and Adriana loves working with her. See you both next time."
Natural service/town words are fine when the reviewer mentioned them; never bolt on keywords they didn't.

## 3★ — thank + probe
Something was off. Thank them, acknowledge the gap without defensiveness, invite the specific detail offline.
> "Thanks for the honest feedback, Mike. Sounds like we were solid on the work but the scheduling back-and-forth wasn't smooth — if you're open to it, call me at [number], I'd like to hear what happened."

## 1★ / 2★ — the recovery flow (owner approval REQUIRED before posting)
1. **Cool-down:** never reply same-hour. Draft, sit, get owner sign-off.
2. **Public reply, once:** calm, factual, human. Acknowledge → one-sentence context (no blow-by-blow) → take it offline with a real contact.
> "Hi [name] — this isn't the experience we want anyone to have. I've looked into what happened on [date] and I'd like to make it right. Please call me directly at [number]. — [owner]"
3. **Private outreach:** the actual fix happens by phone/text. Draft talking points for the owner: what went wrong, what we'll do, by when.
4. **Never** ask them to change the review as a condition of the fix. If genuinely resolved, one soft mention is acceptable: "if you feel the update changed your experience, you're welcome to update the review — either way we're glad it's fixed."
5. **Fake/violating reviews:** don't engage the content. Reply once neutrally ("we have no record of serving you — please contact us if this is an error"), flag to Google citing the specific policy, log it.

## Escalation rule (put in the client doc)
Anything ≤3★, anything mentioning damage/injury/refund/legal, anything about a named employee → owner sees it BEFORE any reply is drafted.
