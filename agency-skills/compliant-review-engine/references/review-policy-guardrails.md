# Review Policy Guardrails

Google policy reference: https://support.google.com/contributionpolicy/answer/7400114

## Hard bans (violating these risks profile suspension — worse than any bad review)
- **Review gating:** filtering or pre-screening customers by satisfaction before showing the review link. Everyone gets the same ask.
- **Incentives:** discounts, entries, freebies for reviews — in ANY direction, including "for honest reviews."
- **Fake/seeded reviews:** employees, family, reciprocal review swaps, purchased reviews.
- **Discouraging negatives:** "only review us if you were happy," intercept pages, satisfaction surveys that route unhappy customers away from Google while routing happy ones to it (that's gating with extra steps).
- **Review-count promises to clients:** velocity depends on their customers; promise the SYSTEM, not a number.

## What's allowed and effective
- Asking every customer, promptly, with a direct link.
- Asking in person then following up by text.
- One reminder. (More reads as pestering and generates spite reviews.)
- Replying to every review.
- Flagging genuinely policy-violating reviews for removal (fake, off-topic, competitor sabotage, doxxing) — cite the specific policy clause; expect Google to decline most.

## Reply constraints
- Never confirm someone is/was a customer in sensitive contexts.
- Never share job details a reasonable customer would consider private.
- Never argue, never blame, never match a reviewer's heat.
- Legal caution: no admissions of fault in public replies for damage/injury disputes — "we'd like to make this right" not "we shouldn't have done X."
