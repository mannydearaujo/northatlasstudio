# Specificity Rules — The Rewrite Method

## The competitor test
Read each sentence and ask: could this appear verbatim on a competitor's site? If yes, it carries zero information. Replace it with something only THIS business can say — or delete it.

## The swap tables (pattern → fix)

| AI filler | Human replacement source |
|---|---|
| "Fast, reliable service" | The actual turnaround: "Cut-and-drop orders go out same-day if you call before noon." |
| "Experienced professionals" | The actual humans: "Hayra's been grooming doodles for nine years." |
| "Affordable pricing" | The actual logic: "Most 5-inch jobs run $8–12 per foot depending on fascia condition." |
| "We understand your needs" | The actual situation: "Contractors call us when the supplier's two weeks out and the job's on Friday." |
| "Serving the greater Boston area" | The actual towns: "Lexington, Bedford, Burlington, and 19 nearby towns." |
| "Your satisfaction is our priority" | The actual policy: "If a seam leaks in the first year, we come back and fix it. No charge." |

## Rewrite sequence (per flagged passage)
1. Identify what the sentence is TRYING to claim.
2. Find the real, verifiable specific behind it (from the voice pack proof-points, the site's own facts, or ask the owner). No specific available → cut the sentence; don't launder vagueness into new vagueness.
3. Rewrite in owner cadence (voice pack `tone.md` calibration lines).
4. Read-aloud check: sayable in one breath, by a person, on a job site.

## Rhythm repair
- Vary paragraph lengths deliberately: a one-sentence paragraph after two long ones lands.
- Break any sentence with 3+ commas unless it's a genuine list.
- Sentence fragments are legal. Sometimes right.
- Replace "Additionally/Furthermore" with nothing — adjacency does the job.

## What NOT to touch
- Verified facts, prices, service names, town names — style surgery only.
- H1/H2 structure and answer-first blocks (SEO/AI-extractability is preserved, not sanded off).
- The client's mandated phrases (service-language.md) and legal/policy language.
