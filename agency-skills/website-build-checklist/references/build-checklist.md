# North Atlas Website Build Checklist

## Phase 1: Intake And Access

- Business name, canonical domain, service area, services, and primary lead action confirmed.
- Brand assets, photos, real proof, testimonials, licenses, hours, and contact details collected.
- CMS/host/domain/DNS, GA4, GTM, Search Console, GBP, booking tool, CRM/form tool access requested.
- Existing site crawled or saved before replacing anything.
- No unverified claims added to the brief.

## Phase 2: Architecture

- Homepage, services hub, real service pages, service-area page, about/trust, FAQ, contact/quote, and 404 planned.
- Future town pages planned only after verified local details exist.
- URL format chosen before build; internal links, canonicals, and sitemap use the same format.
- Navigation has Home, Services, Who We Help or Service Area, About, FAQ, and primary CTA.
- Each page has one job: understanding, trust, action, or follow-up.

## Phase 3: Copy And Content

- Each page has one H1, a clear first-screen answer, and plain owner-friendly language.
- Service pages answer who it is for, what is included, pricing factors, process, proof, FAQ, and CTA.
- About page includes real team/company trust signals without invented biographies.
- FAQ answers match real customer concerns and avoid schema-only questions.
- Copy passes `human-copy-editor` before launch.

## Phase 4: SEO And Schema

- Every indexable page has a unique title, meta description, self-canonical, OG/Twitter metadata, and sitemap entry.
- Bilingual pages use reciprocal `hreflang` with `en-US`, `pt-BR`, and `x-default`.
- JSON-LD matches visible page content: Organization/ProfessionalService, WebPage, Service, FAQPage only where visible, and BreadcrumbList.
- `robots.txt` allows the site and references the production sitemap.
- Search Console property is verified before sitemap submission.

## Phase 5: Conversion And Tracking

- Primary CTA is visible above the fold and repeated after major sections.
- Phone, quote, booking, email, SMS, and form actions are easy on mobile.
- Forms have labels, minimal fields, success/error states, and a real destination.
- GA4 installed once; real lead events fire for phone taps, form submits, booking starts, quote requests, and high-intent clicks.
- Secondary and diagnostic events are separated from headline leads.

## Phase 6: Design, Mobile, And Performance

- Mobile nav opens/closes cleanly, tap targets are usable, and no content overlaps at 375px.
- Text contrast passes practical readability on real backgrounds.
- Images have alt text, stable dimensions, and are compressed.
- Fonts, scripts, and third-party widgets are limited to what the site needs.
- 404 page, favicon, social image, and footer links are present.

## Phase 7: Launch And Post-Launch

- Production domain, HTTPS, redirects, canonical URLs, sitemap, robots, forms, GA4, and schema checked live.
- Sitemap submitted in Search Console and key pages requested for indexing.
- GBP website and appointment links updated when a profile exists.
- Launch notes record what changed, what was submitted, and what still needs owner input.
- First report explains leads, tracking, Search Console status, and next fixes without promising rankings.
