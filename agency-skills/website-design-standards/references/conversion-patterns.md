# Conversion Patterns

## Above the fold (every page, both viewports)
- H1 answers "what + where" in plain words. Subline: for whom / the differentiator.
- Primary CTA visible without scrolling, styled as THE button (brand color, ≥48px tall, verb-first label: "Get a Quote", "Book Now", "Call (781) …").
- Phone number visible in the header — tap-to-call on mobile, real digits on desktop (people on desktops dial from their phone while reading).

## Trust strip (directly under hero)
Stars + review count + years + licensed/insured. Small, factual, no fluff. This single row does more than any paragraph of "why choose us."

## CTA rhythm
- A conversion opportunity after every major proof or explanation section — the reader should never have to scroll UP to act.
- Same primary action all the way down (don't alternate call/book/quote as the primary — pick one per `lead-tracking-installer`'s conversion definition; others are secondary links).
- Final CTA band before the footer: inverted background, one line of copy, the button, the phone.

## Sticky mobile CTA bar (phone-first businesses)
Bottom-fixed bar: Call / Text / Book (the Golden Paws pattern). Rules: ≤64px tall, doesn't cover content endings (padding-bottom on body), each target ≥48px, GA4-tagged per event conventions. This is often the single highest-converting element on the site.

## Forms (quote/contact)
- Fields: name, phone, town, message. Each field beyond these must justify itself with a business reason.
- Labels above fields (not placeholder-only), correct `type=`/`inputmode=`/`autocomplete=`, single column, one CTA button with a specific label ("Send my quote request").
- Above the form: response-time expectation. After submit: confirmation with what-happens-next (and the GA4 event fires here).
- Never a CAPTCHA a human has to solve on a local quote form — use honeypots/server checks.

## Anti-patterns (reject on sight)
Hero carousels/sliders · autoplay video with sound · popups within 10s of landing · chat widgets nobody answers · "Learn more" as a primary CTA label · social icons in the header (they leak visitors) · walls of badges/awards nobody verifies · parallax/scroll-jacking on content sites.
