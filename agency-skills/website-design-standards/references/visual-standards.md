# Visual Standards

## Hierarchy (the 3-second test)
A first-time visitor must know within 3 seconds: what the business does, where, and what to tap. If anything competes with those three answers above the fold, demote it.
- One visual focal point per screen; size/weight/color contrast create order — biggest thing on the page = most important thing.
- F-pattern for text-heavy sections, single-column flow on mobile. Never make the eye zigzag.

## Typography
- Two typefaces max (one is fine): a display face for headings, a workhorse for body. System stacks are legitimate for static local sites.
- Type scale (1.25 ratio works): body 16–18px (never below 16px on mobile), h3 ~1.25×, h2 ~1.56×, h1 ~1.95–2.44×.
- Line height: body 1.5–1.7; headings 1.1–1.3. Line length: 45–75 characters — constrain text columns (`max-width: 65ch`).
- No justified text, no all-caps body, no light weights (<400) on body copy.

## Spacing
- Pick a base unit (8px) and stick to multiples — irregular spacing is what makes DIY sites feel off.
- Sections separated by clear space (64–96px desktop, 40–64px mobile); related elements close, unrelated far (proximity = meaning).
- Whitespace is trust. Cramped = cheap.

## Color & contrast
- One brand color for the primary CTA and ONLY the primary CTA — its color should appear nowhere else at that intensity. One accent, neutrals for everything else.
- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥ 3:1 (WCAG AA — non-negotiable on conversion elements).
- Dark text on light background for content sections; inverted sections used sparingly for rhythm (hero, final CTA band).

## Imagery
- Real photos: the owner, the crew, the van, actual jobs, the storefront. A mediocre real photo outsells a perfect stock photo for local trust.
- Consistent treatment (same corner radius, same aspect ratios per component). Faces looking toward content/CTAs, not off-page.
- Every image sized/compressed per `lighthouse-technical-seo-fixer` rules; alt text always.

## What "professional" means for local service (calibration)
Not fancy — ordered. Aligned edges, consistent spacing, readable text, one clear action, real photos, no clutter. Golden Paws' bright-but-disciplined rebrand is the house reference: playful palette, strict grid.
