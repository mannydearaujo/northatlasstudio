# Blog Opportunity Framework

Good topics serve a customer moment on the path to hiring THIS business. Generate candidates in these categories:

## 1. Pre-purchase questions (highest value)
What people ask before requesting a quote: "how much does X cost in [area]", "how long does X take", "X vs Y which do I need", "do I need a permit for X in MA". These are the questions the owner answers on the phone every week — ask the owner for their top 10.

## 2. Preparation & aftercare
"How to prepare your dog for its first grooming", "what to do before the gutter crew arrives". Low competition, high trust-building, natural service-page links.

## 3. Cost & process transparency
The honest cost-factors post for each major service. Cost content converts because everyone else hides pricing. Must use the client's REAL pricing logic (from the owner), not national averages.

## 4. Seasonal & local-event timing
Real seasonal needs in the actual service area ("preparing Lexington gutters for leaf season"). Schedule 4–6 weeks pre-season. Local angle must be verifiable.

## 5. Adjacent decisions
Questions adjacent to the service where the business has real expertise ("boarding vs pet sitter for holiday travel" for a groomer that boards). Only where adjacency is genuine.

## 6. Proof-backed stories
"How we handled X" mini case studies — pull from `case-study-and-proof-builder` material. One real job, photos, what it cost, what it fixed.

## Explicitly out
- National/generic content with no local or business tie.
- Listicle traffic bait unrelated to hiring intent.
- Anything whose local claims can't be verified by the owner.
