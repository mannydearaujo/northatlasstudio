# Post Types And CTAs

## Post types (with copy patterns)

**Service spotlight** — one service, one benefit, one specific detail.
> Pattern: [what it is] + [who it's for / when you need it] + [one concrete detail] + CTA.
> "5-inch seamless gutters cut on-site to the exact length of your fascia — no seams, no leaks at the joints. Most single-family homes done in a day." → *Get Estimate*

**Proof / review spotlight** — verbatim customer quote + one line of context.
> "'They squeezed us in before the holiday and Bella came home looking like a show dog.' — recent Google review. December books up fast." → *Book*

**Seasonal / timely** — real local seasonal need, dated.
> "Leaves are down in Lexington — clogged gutters overflow at the first hard rain. Cut-and-drop orders this week go out same-day." → *Call*

**FAQ answer** — one real customer question, answered in 2–3 sentences. Pull from the site FAQ or actual inquiries.

**Offer / event** — ONLY real offers with terms and end dates from the owner. Include both.

**Update / milestone** — new equipment, new service area, team addition, certification. Sparingly.

## CTA buttons (GBP native)
Call · Book · Request Quote / Get Estimate · Learn More · Order Online / Sign Up (rare for local service)

Match CTA to the client's primary conversion path per `lead-tracking-installer` (phone-first business → Call; booking-system business → Book). Don't rotate CTAs for variety — consistency trains behavior.

## Format rules
- 150–300 characters ideal; front-load the point in the first 100 (mobile truncation).
- One idea per post. No hashtags (GBP ignores them, they look spammy). Max 1 emoji, only if it fits the voice pack.
- Photos: real photos of real work beat stock 100% of the time. Name the specific photo needed.
- Links: UTM-tag site links (`utm_source=gbp&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=[type]`) so GA4 attributes GBP traffic.

## Cadence defaults
- Retainer standard: 2/week. Minimum viable: 1/week. Never batch-publish (7 posts on Monday reads as automation).
- Offer posts expire — calendar their takedown/refresh.
